## Practical Advice: Choosing Your Capture Strategy

Selecting the right extension depends entirely on how you process information. Before downloading a tool, evaluate your current workflow against these three common capture methods.

**The Direct Capture Method**
If you use Obsidian as an archive and want full copies of articles offline, you need a direct clipper. The **Official Obsidian Web Clipper** or **MarkDownload** are your best options here. Ensure you configure your templates to automatically pull the URL and capture date into your frontmatter so you can track the provenance of the information.

**The Filtered Read-It-Later Method**
If you suffer from digital hoarding, dumping entire articles into Obsidian will quickly ruin your search results. Instead, use a read-it-later tool like **Readwise Reader**. Clip the article, read it in a dedicated app, highlight only the most critical insights, and let the sync plugin bring *only* those highlights into Obsidian. This keeps your vault lean and highly relevant.

**Template Configuration Tips**
Regardless of the extension you choose, setting up a standardized frontmatter template is essential. A good clip template should include:
- `aliases:` The article title
- `tags:` Both broad categories (e.g., `#productivity`) and specific topics
- `url:` The original source link
- `author:` The writer's name
- `date_clipped:` Today's date

Taking thirty minutes to configure these variables in your extension settings will save you hundreds of hours of manual data entry over the next year.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Do these clippers work on mobile devices?
Most browser extensions are limited to desktop browsers like Chrome, Firefox, and Edge. However, tools like Readwise Reader and the Official Obsidian mobile app utilize native iOS and Android share sheets to achieve the same clipping functionality on mobile.

### How do I stop clippers from capturing ads and sidebars?
Modern clippers use readability engines to identify the main article content and strip out surrounding clutter. If a clipper fails on a specific site, using extensions like MarkDownload allows you to manually select the HTML elements you want to capture, ignoring the rest.

### Can I save YouTube videos to Obsidian?
Yes. The Official Obsidian Clipper and Readwise Reader can extract the metadata, title, and sometimes the transcript or description of a YouTube video, formatting it neatly into a Markdown note for further annotation.

### Will clipping entire articles bloat my Obsidian vault?
Markdown files are incredibly small text files. You could save thousands of full-length articles, and it would barely take up a few megabytes of storage. The real risk of "bloat" is polluting your search results with unread text, which is why read-it-later triage workflows are highly recommended.

### Do I need Obsidian Sync to use a web clipper?
No. Clippers like MarkDownload save files directly to your local hard drive directory, while the Official Clipper routes data through the Obsidian URI protocol. Neither requires a paid sync subscription to function on a single device.
